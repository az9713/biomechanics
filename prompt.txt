## Core Framing

Treat the human body as a coupled living machine:

* bones = load-bearing beams, shells, composite structures, and rigid links
* joints = constrained mechanical interfaces with finite degrees of freedom
* cartilage = hydrated, charged, porous, lubricated, load-distributing tissue
* ligaments = passive nonlinear stabilizing constraints
* tendons = elastic and viscoelastic energy-storage elements
* fascia = distributed force-transmission tissue
* muscles = nonlinear chemo-electro-mechanical actuators
* feet and hands = high-degree-of-freedom contact and manipulation systems
* sensory organs = noisy biological measurement systems
* nervous system = feedback/feedforward controller
* metabolism = energy supply system
* cells = adaptive mechanochemical regulators
* whole body = nonlinear, underactuated, constrained multibody dynamical system under gravity

Do not restrict the course to physics only.

Use physics as the mathematical backbone, but whenever chemistry, biology, physiology, neuroscience, materials science, fluid mechanics, electromechanics, continuum electromechanics, control theory, or systems biology is required, bring that knowledge in rigorously.
